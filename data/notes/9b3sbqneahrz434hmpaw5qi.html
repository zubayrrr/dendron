SIGTERM

  • Areas: Linux

The SIGTERM signalis sent to a process to request its termination. Unlike the SIGKILL (Private) signal, it can be caught and interpreted or ignored by the process. This allows the process to perform nice termination releasing resources and saving state if appropriate. SIGINT (Private) is nearly identical to SIGTERM.
