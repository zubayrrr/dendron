Be careful, however, lest this reading of many authors

"Be careful, however, lest this reading of many authors and books of every sort may tend to make you discursive and unsteady. You must linger among a limited number of master-thinkers, and digest their works if you would derive ideas which shall win firm hold in your mind. Everywhere means nowhere. (...) And in reading of many books is distraction..." - Seneca (Private)
