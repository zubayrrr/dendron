Falsifiability

  • Areas: Science

The Falsification Principle, proposed by Karl Popper, is a way of demarcating science from non-science. It suggests that for a theory to be considered scientific it must be able to be tested and conceivably proven false. For example, the hypothesis that "all swans are white," can be falsified by observing a black swan. For Popper, science should attempt to disprove a theory, rather than attempt to continually support theoretical hypotheses.
