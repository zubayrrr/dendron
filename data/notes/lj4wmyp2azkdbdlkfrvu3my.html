Yours &c

  • Areas: Grammar

Sometimes used as "I am \&c"

"\&c" is shorthand for etc. or et cetera, is used in a valediction (Private) by a writer (letter or novel)

"Yours, etc." is used historically for abbreviated endings. It can be found in older newspaper letters to the editor, and often in US legal correspondence. "\&c." may be seen as an alternative abbreviation of et cetera, the ampersand functioning as a ligature form of "et". "I am, etc." and "I remain, etc." are also used.—via Valediction - Wikipedia

Yours truly; Truly yours; Respectfully yours; Yours respectfully; or, if it is desired to give more emphasis, the adverb very may be added, as, Very truly yours; Yours very respectfully.

—via Meaning of the valediction "Yours, \&c." - English Language & Usage Stack Exchange
